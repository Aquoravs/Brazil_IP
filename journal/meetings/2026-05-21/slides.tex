\begin{frame}{Tasks done}
\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{1.2em}
\item \textbf{Which political office moves local economic composition?}
      Instead of using Mayor-crossed channels by assumption, I verify which of them actually move sector shares.
\item \textbf{Updated version of the AR test.}
      The AR test now includes an instrumented version of volume controls and is also evaluated for sector by size groups.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

% ------------------------------------------------------------------
\begin{frame}{Summary of findings}
\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{0.7em}
\item \textbf{Sector-share first stage.} At BNDES aggregate sector every channel's instrument block moves only the Infrastructure share (SW $F$ from 10 to 33); the Agriculture and Industry shares are never identified. At the finer 12-group margin relevance is weak.
\item \textbf{Volume first stage.} Mayor and Mayor\,$\cdot$\,Governor move the volume control.
\item \textbf{AR test, policy block.} Stack carrying the Governor block tend to reject the null of no composition effect, and the rejection survives instrumenting volume.
\item \textbf{AR test, 12 groups.} Governor, President, and Governor\,$\cdot$\,President reject, but on weak first stages --- read as a possible exclusion/direct-effect warning, not as a composition-channel finding.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}
