\section{The estimating equation}\label{sec:eq}

Every cell on the slides comes from one cluster-robust OLS regression at the
municipality--year level:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:rf}
  \log\mathrm{GDP}_{mt}
  \;=\;
  \alpha_m + \delta_t
  \;+\; \gamma^{c\prime}\,Z^{c}_{mt}
  \;+\; \tog{\psi^{c\prime}\,\EC^{c}_{mt}}
  \;+\; \tog{\lambda\,\Vol_{mt}}
  \;+\; \eta_{mt}.
\end{equation}
This is the AR \emph{reduced form}: the GDP equation with the sector
employment-share vector $s_{mt}$ substituted out through its first stage on the
instruments. The exposure controls
$\EC^{c}_{mt}$ and the volume control $\Vol_{mt}$ are toggled on and off
across the four control specifications of \autoref{sec:specs}; the instruments
$Z^{c}_{mt}$ and the fixed effects $\alpha_m, \delta_t$ enter every regression.

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,nosep]
\item $Z^{c}_{mt}$ stacks the $K-1$ retained sector instruments for alignment channel
  $c$; $\EC^{c}_{mt}$ stacks the matching $K-1$ exposure controls. Both are
  defined in \autoref{sec:vars}.
\item $\alpha_m,\delta_t$ are municipality and year fixed effects. Standard
  errors are clustered by municipality.
\item The reported \textbf{AR joint $F$} is the cluster-robust Wald statistic
  for $\gamma^{c}=\bzero$---the joint significance of \emph{channel $c$'s
  instruments only}. Under the optimality null $H_0:\beta=\bzero$ this Wald
  test is the AR test.
  $\EC^{c}_{mt}$ and $\Vol_{mt}$ are partialled out, not tested.
\item This is a \emph{per-channel} run: each regression carries one channel's
  instruments, not a stacked four-channel set.
\end{itemize}

\section{The four channels}\label{sec:channels}

Each alignment channel $c$ pairs a set of political offices with an alignment shock
$\Align^{c}_{mpt}$. The cross-office shocks are products of tier-specific
alignment indicators.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
Channel & Offices & $\Align^{c}_{mpt}=1$ when party $p$\ldots \\
\midrule
M       & mayor                  & holds the mayoralty of $m$ \\
M$\cdot$P   & mayor, president   & holds the mayoralty \emph{and} is in the presidential coalition \\
M$\cdot$G   & mayor, governor    & holds the mayoralty \emph{and} is in the governor's coalition of $s(m)$ \\
M$\cdot$G$\cdot$P & mayor, gov., pres. & is aligned at all three tiers simultaneously \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section{Variable construction}\label{sec:vars}

\subsection*{Outcome --- $\log\mathrm{GDP}_{mt}$}
Municipal log GDP (at 2018 prices), 2002--2017.

\subsection*{Instrument --- $Z^{c}_{jmt}$ (muni-relative owner share)}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,nosep]
\item \textbf{Primitive: owner-party counts.} $L_{f,p,s}$ is the number of
  owners of firm $f$ affiliated with party $p$ in year $s$. Owners with no party affiliation are
  dropped: the denominator counts politically-affiliated owner-years
  only.
\item \textbf{Pre-earliest-election window.}
  $T^{c}_{t}=[\,e^{\min}_{c}(t)-4,\ e^{\min}_{c}(t)-1\,]\cap[2002,2017]$, where
  $e^{\min}_{c}(t)$ is the most recent election, on or before $t$, among the
  offices in channel $c$ (mayoral elections 2000/04/08/12/16; coincident
  governor and president 2002/06/10/14). The window closes before any office in
  the channel can turn over, so the weights are predetermined relative to the
  shock.
\item \textbf{Owner-share weight.}
  \[
    \tilde w^{c}_{jmp,t}
    \;=\;
    \frac{\bar L^{c}_{jmp,t}}{\bar L^{c,\mathrm{affil}}_{m,t}}
    \;=\;
    \frac{\displaystyle\sum_{s\in T^{c}_{t}}\ \sum_{f\in\cF(j,m)} L_{f,p,s}}
         {\displaystyle\sum_{j'}\sum_{p'}\ \sum_{s\in T^{c}_{t}}\
          \sum_{f\in\cF(j',m)} L_{f,p',s}},
  \]
  the share of municipality $m$'s pre-window affiliated owner-years that sit in
  sector $j$ at party $p$. The numerator $\bar L^{c}_{jmp,t}$ is the count of
  pre-window owner-years in cell $(j,m)$ affiliated with party $p$ --- the
  firm-level counts $L_{f,p,s}$ of step~1 summed over the firms ever active in
  sector $j$ of municipality $m$, $\cF(j,m)$, and over the window years
  $s\in T^{c}_{t}$. The denominator $\bar L^{c,\mathrm{affil}}_{m,t}$ sums that
  numerator over all sectors $j'$ and parties $p'$ in $m$. The muni-level
  normalization carries information about each cell's \emph{size} in the local
  economy, which a within-cell intensity weight discards.
\item \textbf{Shift and stack.}
  $Z^{c}_{jmt}=\sum_{p}\tilde w^{c}_{jmp,t}\,\Align^{c}_{mpt}$, then reshaped to
  one column per retained sector.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Exposure control --- $\EC^{c}_{jm,t}$}
The same weights, summed over parties \emph{without} the shift:
\[
  \EC^{c}_{jm,t}\;=\;\sum_{p}\tilde w^{c}_{jmp,t},
\]
cell $(j,m)$'s share of the municipality's affiliated owner mass. It absorbs
the share-component confounding of the shift-share instrument. One column per retained sector per
channel.

\subsection*{Volume control --- $\Vol_{mt}$}
\[
  \Vol_{mt}
  \;=\;
  \frac{\mathrm{BNDES\_total}_{mt}}{\mathrm{GDP}_{m,2002}},
\]
represents the current-year total municipal BNDES disbursements over \emph{initial-period}
(2002) municipal GDP. The denominator is fixed pre-treatment, so $\Vol_{mt}$
varies in $t$ only through the numerator.
