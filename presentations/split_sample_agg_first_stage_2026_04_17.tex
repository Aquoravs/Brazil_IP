\begin{frame}[t]{Summary: What the Split Reveals}
\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{0.9em}
  \item \textbf{Coalition alignment is a valid instrument only in large cities.}
    With muni$\times$year FE (MxJ+MxT), the governor instrument reaches $F=23.5$
    in top-quartile municipalities vs.\ $F=0.4$ in the bottom three quartiles.
    The \emph{full-sample} $F$ was only $2.2$ --- large-city signal was being
    diluted by the majority of small municipalities contributing noise.

  \item \textbf{Party alignment (MxJ+JxT): joint combo significant in large cities
    only.}
    The M+G+P bundle reaches $F=14.6$ in top-quartile cities vs.\ $F=1.3$
    elsewhere.  Yet the full-sample mayor instrument alone shows $F=12.4$ under
    this FE structure, with neither subsample reproducing it individually ---
    suggesting the pooled estimate conflates distinct mechanisms across city sizes.

  \item \textbf{BNDES Share: no first-stage signal in either subsample.}
    No specification exceeds $F=7$ for the BNDES-share outcome in top-quartile
    or bottom-three-quartile municipalities, consistent with the full-sample
    picture.  The alignment instruments do not predict the \emph{within-sector}
    intensive margin of BNDES credit.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}
