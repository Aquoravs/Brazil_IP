\begin{frame}{Main Findings}
\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{0.4em}
\item \textbf{OLS:} Sector composition strongly predicts municipal GDP. Agriculture and Industry shares are positively associated with output; total loan volume is negligible.
\item \textbf{IV:} The joint composition effect does not reject the null across most specifications. However, Industry shows individually significant positive coefficients (5\%) in Governor and Mayor+Governor tiers. Total lending also shows no consistent effect.
\item Findings hold across both classification schemes (broad sectors and firm-size groups) and weighting approaches.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Specification Overview}
\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{0.35em}
\item \textbf{Outcome:} Log municipal GDP (municipality $\times$ year panel, 2002--2017).
\item \textbf{Two sector classifications:}
  \begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{0.2em}
  \item Broad policy sectors: Agriculture, Industry, Infrastructure, Services.
  \item Firm-size groups: Micro ($\leq$9), Small (10--49), Medium (50--249), Large ($\geq$250).
  \end{itemize}
\item \textbf{Four specifications:}
  \begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{0.2em}
  \item Pure OLS: use observed sector shares and total volume.
  \item Composition IV: instrument sector shares only; control for total volume.
  \item Full IV: instrument both sector shares and total volume jointly.
  \item Volume IV: instrument total volume only; use observed sector shares.
  \end{itemize}
\item All specifications include municipality and year fixed effects.
\item The highest mean-share category within each classification is the reference group.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Some Observations}
\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{0.35em}
\item The weights used were selected after comparing multiple candidates.
\item The production weight (owner-count intensity, full-municipality denominator) achieved the highest and most stable Kleibergen--Paap (KP) F-statistic across control specifications ($\approx$60).
\item Data concerns
  \begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{0.2em}
  \item The Agriculture sector has a lower firm-match rate with owner data (19\%) than other sectors (67--95\%), suggesting possible selection in this industry.
  \item 14.6\% of BNDES loan observations (2.5\% by loan value) cannot be matched to RAIS employment data and fall outside the estimation sample.
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Finalized Weights for the Instruments}
\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{0.35em}
\item \textbf{Production weight (primary):} Weight each municipality--party cell by the number of politically affiliated firm owners, divided by the total owner count across \textit{all} firms in the municipality.
\item \textbf{Robustness weight (complement):} Weight by whether a firm has \textit{any} aligned owner (a binary yes/no), divided by the total number of firms in the municipality.
\item Both weights use the full local firm universe as the denominator --- including firms that never received BNDES credit.
\item The robustness weight captures the extensive margin of alignment (some affiliated owner). The production weight captures the intensive margin (more affiliated owners = stronger signal).
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Instrument Strength and the AR Test}
\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{0.35em}
\item \textbf{Total lending instrument} (Volume IV): the instrument for total BNDES volume achieves KP F $\approx$60 (production) and $\approx$48 (robustness) under FE. Relevance is strong.
\item \textbf{Sector-composition instruments} (Composition IV): per-variable first-stage F ranges 1.9--4.8.
\item The composition Wald F reported on the result slides is the joint test of instrument relevance for the composition system.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Bottom Line}
\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{0.5em}
\item \textbf{OLS tells a misleading story.} Sector composition correlates strongly with GDP in OLS because thriving municipalities attract specific types of lending --- not because sectoral reallocation causes growth.
\item \textbf{IV breaks the joint correlation.} Once composition is instrumented with political alignment shocks, the \textit{joint} composition effect drops to zero across both classification schemes and both weighting approaches. Individual Industry coefficients remain significant at 5\% in some tier specifications (Governor, Mayor+Governor under Composition IV and Full IV), a nuance worth investigating further.
\item \textbf{Total lending is also not the driver.} Even when total BNDES volume is instrumented (Volume IV), there is no consistent positive effect on municipal GDP.
\item \textbf{Implication.} Political alignment shapes \textit{who receives} credit, but the sectoral and size composition of that credit does not independently predict local economic outcomes once the endogeneity is removed.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Technical Diagnostics}
\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{0.4em}
\item Omitted reference category (owner-count production weight, Broad Policy Sectors): Services.
\item Omitted reference category (owner-count production weight, Firm-Size Groups): Micro.
\item Omitted reference category (aligned-firm robustness weight, Broad Policy Sectors): Services.
\item Omitted reference category (aligned-firm robustness weight, Firm-Size Groups): Micro.
\item Share of BNDES lending not classified by firm size: 20.9\%.
\item The instrument for total lending is constructed independently from the sector instruments (average correlation with row sum = 1.000).
\item Agriculture sector firm-match rate: 19\% (vs.\ 67--95\% for other sectors) --- possible selection in this industry.
\item 14.6\% of BNDES loan records (2.5\% by loan value) have no RAIS employment match and fall outside the estimation sample.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}
